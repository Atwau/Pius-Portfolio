Freelancer

Most importasnt to me has been a point where I have come to realize that learning never stops Completing my first degree in bachelor in medicine and bachelor in surgery to me was the beginning of my learning curve.

During my training, I have always had change of interests every now and then. My first inspiration during my third year was to do paediatrics for my masters. However, after going through paediatrics rotation, I am sure I did not get any inspiration from anyone. So that dream just died there and then. Nevertheless, I had come from home with my dad's desire for me to be a surgeon, specifically an orthopaedic surgeon. My experience with my first orthopaedic surgery observation in theatre totally knocked me out of that game of orthopaedic surgery. I am not that aggressive. I like precision and minimal cutting. My experience in in internal medicine during my third year was not also interesting to me. Obstetrics and gynaecology has Never been my thing, so i rotated in obstetrics and surgery as part of the course requirement

I moved on to my 4th year, a year full of clinics. The only clinic I got involved so much in was ophthalmology. this was not because i wanted to, but there existed a problem i had experience in person and in my family lineague, LOW VISION. I took of some time to test my vision before the rotation ended, and truly i had low vision. Together with my colleagues we organized a campaign to create awareness on blindness. It was only three month long, but it was just enough to connect us to a number of eye health professionals.It was during this time that I got involved in the activities of eye health africa.I assumed the role of content administrator for WCEA platform for eye health africa. I also volunteered to run the youtube channel for eye health africa. I edited videos, helped in digital presence. I also got to know Dr Paula Atubadzi, a Ghana, ophthalmologist and we collaborated in eye health activities.this I did through my fifth year

My pre intersship time was a time when I was in the cross roads. Thanks be to mentorship. I had gotten to know Dr Solomon Ondoma, aneurosurgeon at MercyOne hospital inthe USA. I interacted with him on several occasions and shared my personal story with him. This is where my journey in the field of neurosurgery started
